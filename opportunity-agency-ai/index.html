Opportunity [Agency AI]

An agent organization
of agent organizations.

Where a single Hunter AI — like Crypto Hunter AI — is a multi-agent organization that hunts opportunity in one domain, Opportunity is the layer above them: it reads across every independent Hunter engine and decides how your finite time and money should actually be spent today, across every domain at once. Built on Veritas — the deterministic-scaffold framework every engine, and Opportunity itself, are made of.

The stack

Three layers. Veritas is infrastructure, not a product.

Veritas is the tool stack — the org registry, the hub, the propose → gate → debate mechanics. Hunter engines are independent org instances built on it, one per domain: each owns its own scouts, its own deterministic gate, its own debate, its own memory. Opportunity reads every engine's verified output through read-only bridges and runs its own gather → weigh → learn agents on top — not deciding what's true (each engine's gate already settled that), but what's worth this user's next dollar and next hour.

Two principles, every layer

The arc is the validation

Scouts and debaters run unconstrained; the gate fires at discrete points only — once on filing, again only on new evidence. Never continuously. True inside an engine and at the Opportunity level.

Gate-hard vs. debate-argued differs per domain

Decided by how deterministically checkable a risk is. Crypto's scam-safety is gate-hard. Collectibles' arbitrage math is gate-hard; authenticity is debate-argued instead. No universal split.

Two kinds of debate. Within an engine, Advocate/Skeptic/Strategist contest one opportunity's own merit — never changing the verdict. Across engines, at the Opportunity level, the debate isn't about truth at all (each engine's gate already settled that) — it's about priority: independently-legitimate opportunities from different domains competing for the same finite time and money.

Constitution

Hard invariants, every layer.

Never holds keys

Never signs, executes, or submits anything on the user's behalf — at any layer.

Deterministic trust, at every level

A gate decides what's verified inside an engine; one deterministic allocation decides what reaches the user at the Opportunity level. Arbitration, not another opinion.

Provenance, always

Every trusted claim traces all the way down to the engine and gate check that produced it.

Intelligence, not advice

Opportunity ranks and arbitrates what's real; the user decides what to act on — across financial, legal, and personal-risk domains at once.
